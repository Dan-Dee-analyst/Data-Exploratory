<h1>Electric Vehicle in the U.S.</h1>

 ### https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/fatmanur12/electric-vehicle-population/data

<h2>Description</h2>
Dataset of registered electric vehicles in the U.S. including manufacturer, model year, registration state, electric vehicle type and eligibility for clean-fuel incentives.

<h2>Problem Statement:</h2>
Electric vehicle (EVs) has become an important factor in reshaping the transportation and energy sectors. With increasing emphasis on sustainability, electric vehicle adoption is accelerating but not evenly across geography or manufacturers. This project investigates the distribution of electric vehicles by state, manufacturer, year of make and model. The primary goal is to identify adoption treads, regional gaps and the most dominant vehicle makes.
Using this dataset, we analysed factors such as model, make, type of EV and registration state to derive insights about U.S. EV adoption patterns.

<h2>Business Impact:</h2>
Analysing the spread and adoption of EVs across the United States can help in guiding investments and infrastructure planning for automakers, utility providers and consumers. By identifying where adoption is growing quickly, stakeholders can better deploy EV incentives, manufacture resources and charging infrastructure. This data can also reveal opportunities for expansion in underserved markets and inform consumer behaviour predictions.

<h2>Dataset Details:</h2>

- <b>Rows: 130,000</b> 
- <b>Columns: 10</b> 
- <b>Size: 1,200 kb (1.2 mb)</b> 
- <b>Source: Electric Vehicle population</b> 

<h2>Target Features:</h2>

<b>Key features are</b>

- <b>	State:- Determines geographic concentration of EVs</b>
- <b>	Make:- Identify popular and rising manufacturers</b>
- <b>	Electric vehicle type: Differences between Battery electric vehicle (BEV) and plug-in-hybrid (PHEV)</b>
- <b>	Model year: - used to analyse adoption over year</b>
- <b>	Electric Range: provide more details into technological performance</b>
- <b>	CAFV Eligibility : Assesses policy-driving of electric vehicles</b>

<h2>List of Analysis:</h2>

<h4> 1.	Total Records</h4>
To establish the scale of the dataset which a total of 130,000 records were reviewed. After initial filtering to remove entries with missing states and make values, it turned out 125,000 records remained usable for analysis.

<h4> 2.	Records by model year</h4> 
Grouping records by model year reveals a relevant increase in EV registrations beginning around 2017, with an exponential rise through 2023. A line chart of yearly counts shows an S-shaped adoption curve, which supports the rapid consumer shift to EVs.

- <b>Model years span from 1997 to 2023 with peak registration in 2021-2022.</b>
- <b>Average EV model age is decreasing indicating a growing share of newer vehicles</b>

<h4> 3. EV type & manufacture Distribution</h4> 
The electric vehicle type column shows that 65% of records are BEV and 35% are PHEV, showing consumer and regulatory preference for fully electric models.

- <b>Tesla dominates 50% of all entries in the dataset</b>
- <b>Other common makes: Nissan, chevrolet, ford, BMW, toyota.</b>

<h4> 4.	State level Analysis</h4> 
By aggregating state values:

- <b>Southern and midwestern states show low EV representation</b>
- <b>California leads with over 40% of EVs followed by washington, florida, texas and new york</b>

<h4> 5.	Electric Range and pricing<h4>
Among the 45% of records that include electric range:

- <b>The average range is approximately 230 miles with values from 0 up to 400 miles</b>
- <b>Newer vehicle model tend to have longer ranges</b>
- <b>Where  mid-range pricing ($35,000 - $60,000) suggesting mainstream market targeting</b>

<h2>CONCLUSION</h2>
The electric vehicle market in the u.s. is accelerating quickly with tesla leading the way. Adoption is geographically uneven with califonia leading in registrations. Most vehicles are recent models (2018-2023) with strong growth. While BEVs outnumber PHEVs both show important quantities. Electric range continues to improve with newer models.
These trends offer clean view into where the EV market is heading and can directly inform both the public and private decison-making in this aspect
